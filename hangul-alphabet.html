What is Hangul?

Hangul, the Korean script, was developed by King Sejong the Great in the 15th century, aiming to provide literacy to common people. Before Hangul, Koreans used Classical Chinese characters. Hangul is a phonetic alphabet, consisting of consonants and vowels, making it easy to learn and read. It is considered one of the most scientific writing systems in the world.
